RCD spaces: Splitting theorems
and applications

November 9 - 11, 2021.

About

RCD(K,N) spaces are a synthetic generalization of manifolds with Ricci curvature bounded below and dimension bounded above. They are stable under measured Gromov - Hausdorff convergence, and so are also a generalization of Ricci limit spaces. Several now classical results such as Cheeger-Gromoll's Splitting Theoremand Cheeger-Colding's Almost Splitting Theorem have been generalized to this setting.

In this minischool we will review some of the new proofs and how either the theorems or the ideas behind the proofs have been used to prove other results such as the volume cone implies metric cone (Gigli-De Phillips); stability of the torus (Mondello - Mondino - Perales) and; volume entropy rigidity (Connell - Dai - Nuñez Zimbrón - Perales -Suárez Serrato- Wei)
